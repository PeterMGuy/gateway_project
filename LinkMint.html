Mint Museum Uptown

Being established as North Carolina’s first art museum in 1936, the Mint Museum is noted to be a leading cultural institution and museum of international design and art. With their two locations — Mint Museum Randolph in the center of Eastover and Mint Museum Uptown at Levine Center. Mint Museum Uptown contains the internationally famous Craft + Design collection, coupled along with outstanding collections of American, contemporary, and European art. The building was designed by Machado and Silvetti Associates of Boston. This five-story facility combines intriguing architecture with excellent exhibitions to provide visitors with educational and cultural experiences. Located within the heart of Charlotte’s city center, Mint Museum Uptown is an essential part of the Levine Center for the Arts. When you visit, look forward to seeing various art forms. This is an activity fit for people of all ages.
